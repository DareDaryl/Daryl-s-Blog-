The Crying
Man

I was getting a midday drink at Blancos Tequila when I met the crying man. The bar was a large square with an open space in the middle, allowing everyone seated to face each other. Open air. It was quite nice. I was minding my own business and lost in thought when I looked up and noticed him. He was an extremely handsome man, fit, well dressed, good hair and maybe in his early thirties. He was sitting alone and at first glance, nothing seemed off but when I looked closer, he was sobbing quietly to himself while staring into his drink. My heart immediately went out to him. Did he lose his job? Did a relationship end? Did a relative or pet pass away? I then saw that the couple seated to his right had also noticed the crying man and asked him how he was doing. They even scooted over to sit right next to him and chatted softly. Then a guy who had just ordered a pizza to his left noticed and I saw him slide the pie over to the crying man to ask if he wanted a slice which he accepted. The guy still had a few tears rolling down his cheeks but he was now smiling softly and nodding to his new friends. Something about witnessing these acts of pure human kindness really got to me. No one else noticed these interactions so I felt privvy to a moment of truly goodwill. That day always stuck in my mind and I wonder sometimes how the crying man is doing now.
